Types of Bikes You Should Know

Before buying a bike, it’s important to understand the major categories available. Below is an overview:

  • Road Bikes: Lightweight, fast, and perfect for long-distance paved rides.
  • Mountain Bikes: Built for rugged terrains, with suspension for comfort and control.
  • Hybrid Bikes: A mix of road and mountain features, ideal for casual city rides.
  • Gravel Bikes: Versatile, great for mixed surfaces like dirt, gravel, and pavement.
  • Electric Bikes (E-Bikes): Assisted pedaling with motors, perfect for commuting.

How to Choose the Right Bike for You

  1. Identify your main purpose (commuting, racing, fitness, or adventure).
  2. Set a budget that balances quality and affordability.
  3. Test ride before finalizing to ensure comfort.
  4. Pay attention to frame size and handlebar height.
  5. Consider accessories such as helmets, locks, and lights for safety.

Maintenance Tips Every Rider Should Know

Owning a bike is more than just buying—it requires regular care. Keep your chain lubricated, tires inflated, and brakes checked. Store your bike indoors to avoid rust, and schedule professional servicing once a year for optimal performance.
