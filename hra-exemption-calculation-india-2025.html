HRA Exemption Rules & Calculation (India 2025)

This page explains the three tests used to compute the exempt portion of House Rent Allowance (HRA) and provides worked examples and tips for supporting documents.

Which salary component is used?

Salary for HRA calculation means Basic + Dearness Allowance (if treated as part of salary) — use the employer’s salary breakup from Form 16/pay slip.

Three tests (exempt = minimum of)

  1. Actual HRA received.
  2. 50% (metro) or 40% (non-metro) of salary (Basic + DA).
  3. Rent paid − 10% of salary (Basic + DA).

Worked example

Employee in Mumbai (metro): Basic+DA = ₹40,000; HRA received = ₹18,000; Rent paid = ₹20,000.

  • Actual HRA = ₹18,000
  • 50% of salary = ₹20,000
  • Rent − 10% salary = ₹20,000 − ₹4,000 = ₹16,000

Exempt HRA = minimum(18,000; 20,000; 16,000) = ₹16,000 per month.

Documentation & compliance

  • Keep rent receipts or rent agreement; if annual rent > ₹1,00,000, landlord PAN may be required.
  • If employer asks, submit a rent declaration and rent receipts for the year.

Special cases

If you live in a house you own — you cannot claim HRA. If employer provides company accommodation, HRA rules differ.

Quick HRA checklist

  • Confirm city type (metro vs non-metro).
  • Use correct basic salary figure — include DA if taxable as salary.
  • Keep rent proofs for audit.
Updated 2025. This guide simplifies the legal tests — consult your HR/payroll or tax advisor for edge cases.
