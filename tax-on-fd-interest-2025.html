Tax on FD Interest Income in India (2025)

Interest from bank fixed deposits (FDs) is taxable as income from other sources. This page explains tax treatment, TDS thresholds, exemptions and planning tips.

TDS on FD interest

Banks and scheduled financial institutions deduct TDS at source if aggregate interest paid to an individual in a financial year exceeds the threshold (check current threshold; historically ₹40,000 and lower for senior citizens). If PAN is not furnished, TDS may be at a higher rate.

How to report FD interest

Include total interest from all FDs in ‘Income from Other Sources’ while filing ITR. If TDS was deducted, claim credit for TDS against final tax liability.

Examples & calculations

Suppose you have total FD interest ₹60,000 and your taxable income places you in 20% slab. Tax on interest = 20% of ₹60,000 = ₹12,000 plus cess. Deduct TDS already paid to compute balance payable/refundable.

Planning tips

  • Use Form 15G / 15H (if eligible) to avoid TDS for small taxpayers/senior citizens if total tax liability is nil.
  • Consider tax-saving alternatives (tax-saving FDs under Sec 80C, but these have a lock-in).
  • Stagger maturities across years to manage TDS & tax impact.
Always check the current TDS limits & consult your tax professional for complex cases (multiple banks, joint accounts).
