The Macro Strategy Mechanics: Two 5-Year Blocks

This strategy relies on an active loop process that redirects guaranteed distributions into secondary interest accelerators. Here is how your portfolio operates year-by-year:

Block 1: Years 1 to 5 (The Foundation)
You park your initial ₹3,25,000 in a POMIS account, which shoots out ₹2,004 every single month. You manually take ₹2,000 of that cash flow instantly every 30 days to buy 3-Year Time Deposits (POTD). Because Time Deposits pay out annual interest into your savings account, those distributions compound over the first five years, triggering supplementary ₹1,000 deposits every time your savings threshold clears the marker.
The Year 5 Master Merge (The Accelerator)
At Month 60, your primary POMIS block reaches full maturity. Simultaneously, the dozens of micro 3-Year Time Deposits you opened in Years 1 and 2 have completely matured with full quarterly compounding interest intact. You take all of this accumulated matured capital and merge it back into your principal to launch a brand new, highly inflated POMIS block.
Block 2: Years 6 to 10 (The Explosion)
Because your baseline capital base grew significantly at the Year 5 mark, the secondary POMIS account spits out a far larger monthly dividend. You run the exact same 3-year Time Deposit mapping mechanism on this bigger allocation. By Year 10, the original principal has experienced structural double-compounding, accelerating your absolute simple return up to the 12.30% mark.
