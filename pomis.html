How the 6-Year Portfolio Growth Works (The Calculation)

Let's look at the exact mathematical mechanics behind this manual compounding matrix, assuming an initial deposit of ₹3,25,000:

  • Step 1 (Months 1-60): Your ₹3,25,000 principal in POMIS earns a fixed card rate of 7.40% p.a., producing exactly ₹2,004 every single month.
  • Step 2 (The Primary Loop): Every single month, you manually use the incoming interest to open a fresh 3-Year Time Deposit (POTD) of ₹2,000 at 7.10% p.a. (compounded quarterly). The remaining ₹4 change sits safely in your Post Office Savings Account (SB) earning 4.00% p.a.
  • Step 3 (The Bonus Interest Trigger): Post Office Time Deposits drop their interest payouts annually back into your SB account. As these compounding bonuses accumulate, your SB balance continually crosses the ₹1,000 mark. Per the strategy rules, you immediately use every extra ₹1,000 block to launch supplementary 3-Year TDs.
  • Step 4 (The Year 5 Shift): At month 60, the POMIS account finishes its life cycle. You take your flat ₹3,25,000 principal and secure it into a 1-Year Time Deposit at 6.90% p.a. to keep it active for one final year.
