India’s Role in the G20 Finance Agenda – Priorities, Outcomes & Challenges

Updated: 2025 | By: T24K Finance Desk

India is playing a decisive role in shaping the G20 finance agenda, driving reforms in global taxation, digital finance, climate funding, economic stability and sustainable growth. This article provides a detailed breakdown of India’s priorities, achievements, challenges, and future strategy in the G20 finance track.

1. Why the G20 Finance Agenda Matters for India

The G20 represents the world’s largest economies, responsible for over 85% of global GDP and 75% of global trade. Decisions made here influence:

  • Global tax policy
  • Debt restructuring
  • Climate finance
  • Financial regulations
  • Trade and investment flows

For India, the G20 is a platform to influence global standards while aligning them with domestic interests.

2. India’s Strategic Priorities in the G20 Finance Track

India has been pushing an agenda centered on:

  • Digital financial inclusion
  • Tax transparency and fairness
  • Climate finance for developing countries
  • Debt relief for low-income economies
  • Reform of global financial institutions

The priorities are driven by India’s experience as both a fast-growing economy and a developing nation with complex social challenges.

3. India’s Leadership in Corporate Tax Reform

India has been a vocal supporter of the Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) initiative, aimed at ending tax avoidance by global corporations.

Key contributions:
  • Supporting digital economy taxation
  • Advocating minimum corporate tax rates
  • Pushing multinational transparency
  • Level playing field for emerging markets

Outcomes include the Global Minimum Tax Agreement (15%) adopted by over 130 countries.

4. Digital Public Infrastructure – India’s Global Export

India showcased UPI, Aadhaar and Digital Identity as low-cost, scalable infrastructure models for the world.

Benefits:
  • Financial inclusion for unbanked populations
  • Low-cost digital payments
  • Rapid integration of services
  • Fraud prevention through biometrics

Over 20 countries are studying or adopting the model voluntarily.

5. Climate Finance and Green Investment

India pushed for enhanced climate funding to assist emerging economies in adapting to rising environmental costs.

Key demands:

  • $100 billion annual climate fund
  • Affordable green technology transfers
  • Equitable carbon pricing
  • Financial risk mitigation schemes

Progress has been limited due to political disagreement among developed nations.

6. Debt Crisis Management

India has called for faster debt restructuring mechanisms for vulnerable economies.

7. Domestic Reforms Strengthening India’s Position

  • GST implementation
  • Unified digital payment systems
  • Banking sector recapitalization
  • Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC)

These reforms enhanced India’s negotiation credibility.

9. Challenges Ahead

  • Fiscal deficits
  • Regulatory uncertainty
  • Slow labor reforms
  • Geopolitical tensions
  • Fragmented global cooperation

10. Future Priorities for India

  • Capital market deepening
  • Digital asset regulation
  • Green bond expansion
  • Financial literacy
  • Blockchain-based governance

Conclusion

India has emerged as a major voice in shaping the G20 finance agenda, promoting inclusive, transparent and sustainable economic policies that benefit both developed and emerging economies.
