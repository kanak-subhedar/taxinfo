India’s Role in the G20 Finance Agenda – Priorities, Outcomes & Challenges

Last Updated: 2025   |   By: T24K Finance Desk

The G20 — a global forum of major economies — plays a vital role in shaping the global financial landscape. India has emerged as a major participant, influencing decisions related to tax reform, digital payments, sustainable finance, and financial inclusion.

1. India’s Key Priorities in the G20 Finance Agenda

India has focused on global financial stability, tax transparency, digital inclusion, and climate financing, with a strong emphasis on supporting developing economies.

2. Corporate Tax Reform and BEPS

India has been a key advocate for fair taxation of multinational corporations through the Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) initiative.

3. Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) Innovation

India showcased UPI, Aadhaar, and other digital solutions as models for low-cost financial inclusion worldwide.

4. Climate Finance and Green Investment

Climate finance emerged as a key theme, with India pushing for increased flows into developing economies.

5. Debt Crisis Management for Vulnerable Economies

India advocated a systematic approach to restructuring debt for low-income nations, with faster multilateral coordination.

6. India’s Domestic Reforms Strengthened Its Influence

Major reforms — GST, digital payments, IBC — aligned with global best practices and increased India’s leadership credibility.

7. Challenges and Structural Limitations

Fiscal deficits, slow labor reforms, and regulatory uncertainty remain key challenges.

8. Future Priorities for India

Strategic focus areas include deepening capital markets, digital assets policy, and stronger AML enforcement.
