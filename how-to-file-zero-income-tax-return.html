How to File a Zero Income Tax Return in India — Complete Step-by-Step Guide (2025)

Updated: November 2025 • Estimated read: 12–16 minutes

Quick answer: You can file a zero income tax return (ITR showing zero tax liability) if your total taxable income for the financial year is below the basic exemption limit or if deductions/reliefs reduce taxable income to zero. This guide shows eligibility checks, required documents, exact e-filing steps, sample calculations, and common pitfalls.

Why file a zero income tax return? (Important reasons)

Even when you owe no tax, filing an ITR can be valuable. Filing provides official proof of income and compliance for bank loans, visas, government subsidies and helps avoid notices later. Key reasons to file even if tax is zero:

  • Proof of income for loans or visas.
  • Claiming refunds, if TDS was deducted.
  • Carry forward certain losses (only if return filed within due date).
  • Avoid penalties or notices in some situations (e.g., foreign assets, high-value transactions).

Who can file a zero tax return?

Generally, you can file a zero tax return if any of the following are true for the relevant financial year:

  1. Your total taxable income after deductions is below the basic exemption limit (for FY 2024–25 / AY 2025–26 the limits vary by age and regime — check current slab).
  2. Your tax liability is zero after applying rebates, reliefs and deductible investments (for example under Section 80C, 80D etc.).
  3. Your tax withheld (TDS) exceeds tax payable and you are due a refund — filing ITR is needed to claim refund.

Important: when filing is still recommended even with zero tax

  • If you had TDS or TCS and expect a refund.
  • If you have carried forward losses that require filing to preserve rights.
  • If you have foreign assets, high-value transactions or are a beneficiary of specified financial events (even if income is low).

Step 1 — Quick eligibility checklist (do this first)

Run this checklist before filing:

  • Calculate gross income (salary, interest, capital gains, freelance income, rental, etc.).
  • Apply standard deduction and exemptions (e.g., standard deduction for salaried taxpayers).
  • Claim deductions (80C, 80D, 80TTA etc.).
  • Subtract exemptions to get taxable income. If taxable income ≤ basic exemption limit, you qualify for zero-tax return.
  • Confirm TDS and refund situations.

Step 2 — Documents & information you need

Before filing, gather these items:

  • PAN card details (mandatory).
  • Aadhar (if linked) and other ID proof.
  • Form 16 (if employed) — for salary and TDS summary.
  • Bank statements for interest income.
  • Investment proofs for 80C, 80D, 80TTA etc.
  • Proof of rent paid (if claiming HRA) and rent receipts.
  • Details of any capital gains or other income (if applicable).

Step 3 — Choose the correct ITR form

Which form to use?

  • ITR-1 (Sahaj): For individuals with salary, one house property & other small income (interest), and total income up to the prescribed limit — most common for salaried people.
  • ITR-2: If income includes capital gains, more than one house property or foreign assets — usually not zero-tax simple cases.
  • ITR-4: For presumptive income (business or professional under certain sections).

If your income is strictly salary + small bank interest and you qualify as an individual resident, ITR-1 is typically the simplest choice.

Step 4 — Use e-Filing portal or authorized intermediaries

Options to file electronically:

  1. Income Tax e-Filing portal (https://www.incometax.gov.in/) — official and free.
  2. Authorized e-return intermediaries and aggregate filing portals (fee may apply).
  3. CA/Tax Professional filing on your behalf.

Steps on the official e-Filing portal (concise)

  1. Create or log in to your account at incometax.gov.in.
  2. Go to File Income Tax Return → choose financial year → select ITR form.
  3. Fill in personal details & bank details (IFSC/Account no. for refund).
  4. Enter income details: salary, interest, any other income.
  5. Declare deductions under appropriate sections (80C, 80D etc.).
  6. Validate and preview return — ensure final tax payable shows ₹0.
  7. Submit the return and e-verify (Aadhaar OTP / Netbanking / EVC).

Important: Always e-verify your return within 30 days. Without e-verification, your return is considered not processed and must be physically signed and posted to CPC, Bengaluru — which causes delays.

Step 5 — Common traps & how to avoid them (don’t miss these)

  • Mismatch between Form 16 and return: If Form 16 shows salary and TDS but you enter different values, you may get an INTIMATION. Always use Form 16 figures for salary and TDS inputs.
  • Forgetting interest income: Small bank interest can push you above the exemption limit. Check FD/Recurring and savings interest.
  • Not e-verifying: Without e-verification, filing is incomplete and you may be treated as non-filer.
  • Not updating bank details: If refund is due and IFSC/account is wrong, refunds are delayed.

Step 6 — Sample walkthrough: Filing zero tax return on incometax.gov.in

Below is a concise walkthrough with exact fields you will encounter:

  1. Login → Dashboard → “e-File” → “Income Tax Return”
  2. Select Assessment Year (for FY 2024–25 choose AY 2025–26)
  3. Choose Form: ITR-1 (if eligible)
  4. Personal Info: Check PAN, name, address, Aadhaar.
  5. Bank Details: Add account number and IFSC for refund.
  6. Salary Details: Fill income from Form 16.
  7. Deductions: Add 80C/80D etc., attach proofs offline (no upload required at filing).
  8. Computation: Verify that Tax Payable = 0. If TDS > 0, claim refund details in refund section.
  9. Preview and Submit: Download PDF, e-verify immediately.

Step 7 — If TDS was deducted and you are due a refund

If your employer or bank deducted tax (TDS) but your final liability is zero, file ITR to claim refund. Important details:

  • Enter TDS details exactly as per Form 16/26AS.
  • Set your bank account for refund — refunds are credited to the account you specify and validated by PAN/IFSC.
  • Refund timeline: usually processed in 3–6 weeks after filing and verification, but may take longer if manual scrutiny is triggered.

Edge cases — When zero-tax filing may not be straightforward

These situations require extra care or professional help:

  • Foreign income or assets — disclosure required even if tax is zero.
  • High-value transactions (property sale, sale of shares) — may need ITR even with 0 tax.
  • Multiple employers in year — reconcile Form 16s and salary breakup.
  • Carried forward losses — filing is required to carry losses forward.

Checklist before pressing Submit

  • PAN & Aadhaar details are correct and linked.
  • All incomes are declared (salary, interest) — even small amounts.
  • Deductions are supported by proofs (retain but no upload required).
  • Bank account details for refund are correct.
  • Final tax payable table shows ₹0 and net refund (if any) is correct.
  • Return is e-verified or physical signed copy is posted if necessary.

If everything checks out, submit and e-verify immediately to complete filing.

Sample FAQ (short answers)

Q: Can I file ITR even if I have zero income?

A: Yes. Filing ITR when taxable income is zero is allowed and often recommended for proof of income or to claim refunds.

Q: Do I need to attach documents when e-filing?

A: No, you should retain proofs but not attach them during e-filing. Keep documents for 6 years in case of assessment.

Q: What if I forget to e-verify?

A: The return will be considered not processed. You must e-verify within 30 days or send a signed ITR-V to CPC Bengaluru.
