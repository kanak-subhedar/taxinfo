Step 1 — Understand gross salary vs taxable income

Gross salary usually includes basic pay, dearness allowance (if taxable), HRA, special allowances, and employer contributions (EPF — employer part is not taxable immediately). From gross salary, standard deductions and eligible deductions (like 80C) are deducted to arrive at taxable income.

Step 2 — Key deductions you should know

  • Standard deduction: ₹50,000 (if applicable) — common for salaried taxpayers under the old regime.
  • Section 80C: Up to ₹1,50,000 — PPF, ELSS, EPF employee contribution, life insurance premium, etc.
  • House Rent Allowance (HRA): Exemption depends on your salary structure, rent paid, and city of residence.
  • Section 80D: Health insurance premium deduction (separate from 80C).

New regime comparison

If you choose the new tax regime (which restricts many deductions like 80C), the taxable income may be higher but slabs are lower. For many with small 80C claims, the new regime is better — but with ₹1,00,000 in 80C we probably benefit from the old regime. How to reduce your tax on ₹10 lakh salary

Common, legal ways to reduce taxable income:

  1. Max out Section 80C — invest up to ₹1.5 lakh (PPF, ELSS, EPF contributions).
  2. Plan HRA — claim correct HRA with valid rent receipts.
  3. Claim 80D for health insurance premium.
  4. Start SIPs in ELSS for tax benefit + growth potential.
  5. Use NPS (80CCD) — additional deduction under 80CCD(1B) where applicable.

Checklist before you file your ITR

  • Collect Form 16 and salary breakup from employer.
  • Consolidate proof for 80C, 80D, home loan principal, tuition fees etc.
  • Calculate HRA exemption with rent receipts.
  • Choose tax regime (old or new) after comparison.
  • Use e-filing portal or an accountant if unsure.

Common questions

Q: Should I choose old or new tax regime for ₹10 lakh salary?

A: It depends on your deductions. If you claim substantial 80C, HRA and 80D, often the old regime gives more savings. If you have few deductions and want simplicity, the new regime may be better. Compare both using the calculator on our site: Old vs New tax regime.

Q: Is employer EPF contribution taxable?

A: Employer EPF contribution is generally tax-exempt during employment up to certain limits; the employer contribution beyond specified threshold may attract tax. Employees’ own EPF contribution (under 80C) is eligible for deduction.

Q: How to compute HRA exemption exactly?

A: HRA exemption is the minimum of: (a) actual HRA received, (b) rent paid minus 10% of salary, (c) 50% of salary if living in metro (40% non-metro). See our dedicated HRA guide: HRA exemption calculation.

Q: Does standard deduction apply in both regimes?

A: Standard deduction is available in the old regime. New regime simplifies slabs and disallows several deductions, so standard deduction is not separately applicable there — check the latest official rules.
