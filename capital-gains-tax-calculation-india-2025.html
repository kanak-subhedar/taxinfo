Capital Gains Tax in India (2025) — Short-Term vs Long-Term

Capital gains arise on the sale of capital assets: shares, property, mutual funds, bonds, etc. The holding period determines whether gain is short-term or long-term and which tax rates apply.

Key rules

  • Equity shares / equity mutual funds: STCG if held ≤12 months (taxed at 15% typically); LTCG if >12 months (special exemptions and 10% over ₹1L after April 2018 rule).
  • Immovable property: STCG if ≤24 months (taxed at slab rates), LTCG if >24 months (indexation benefit applies, taxed at 20%).

Indexation & LTCG

For certain assets (like property), you apply cost inflation index to compute indexed cost of acquisition which reduces taxable LTCG.

Exemptions

  • Section 54/54F: reinvestment in residential property can save LTCG tax on sale of property, subject to conditions and timelines.
  • Section 54EC: invest capital gains in specified bonds (NHAI/RRBL) within 6 months, subject to limits.

Worked example (property)

Sale price ₹50L, indexed cost of acquisition = ₹30L → LTCG = ₹20L, tax at 20% → ₹4L + cess. If you reinvest ₹20L in eligible bonds under 54EC, LTCG may be exempt up to that amount.

Capital gains rules change — verify current indexation base & holding period definitions before filing.
