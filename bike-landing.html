Quick Buying Guide (One Page)

  • Fit First: Use the brand’s chart, verify standover, check reach/stack. A short stem or narrower bars can save a mis-size.
  • Tires: Endurance road: 28–32 mm; gravel: 38–45 mm; hybrid: 35–40 mm; MTB: 2.2–2.4”. Wider = comfier.
  • Gearing: Compact (50/34) or 48/31 with 11–34 for hills; 1× on gravel/MTB if you prefer simplicity.
  • Brakes: Hydraulics for power + modulation; mechanical discs if you want easy home servicing.
  • Tubeless: Fewer flats, lower pressure, better grip. Worth it on gravel/MTB and endurance road.
  • Budget: Keep 10–15% for fit tweaks, pedals, lights, and a good floor pump.

FAQ

Aluminum or carbon?

Carbon is lighter and can ride smoother; aluminum is durable and cost-effective. Choose by terrain and goals.

Can one bike do everything?

A gravel bike with two wheelsets (slicks + knobbies) gets close for many riders.

Do I need clipless pedals?

They improve pedaling efficiency; start with dual-sided pedals to retain flat-shoe flexibility.
