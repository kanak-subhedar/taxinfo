Fixed Deposits and Taxation – An Overview

Fixed Deposits (FDs) are one of the most popular savings instruments in India. They provide guaranteed returns and safety of capital, making them ideal for risk-averse investors. However, many people overlook the fact that interest earned on FDs is fully taxable under the Income Tax Act.

How is FD Interest Taxed?

Interest earned on Fixed Deposits is added to your ‘Income from Other Sources’ and taxed according to your income tax slab rate. There is no special tax benefit unless the FD is specifically a Tax-Saving FD under Section 80C.

TDS Rules on Fixed Deposits

  • Banks deduct TDS at 10% if the total FD interest in a year exceeds ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens).
  • If you haven’t submitted your PAN, TDS is deducted at 20%.
  • TDS is deducted annually, even if the FD matures after several years.

How to Avoid Excess TDS?

If your total income is below the taxable limit, you can submit Form 15G (for individuals below 60) or Form 15H (for senior citizens) to your bank. This ensures that TDS is not deducted.

Example of FD Taxation

Suppose you invested ₹5,00,000 in an FD at 7% annual interest. You earn ₹35,000 interest in one year. Since it is below ₹40,000, no TDS is deducted. However, if your total income including this interest exceeds the basic exemption limit, you still need to pay tax while filing your ITR.

Tax-Saving Fixed Deposits

A 5-year Tax-Saving FD qualifies for deduction under Section 80C, up to ₹1.5 lakh per year. However, the interest earned on such FDs is still taxable.

Ways to Save Tax on FD Interest

  • Distribute FD investments across family members in lower tax brackets.
  • Use senior citizen benefits like higher exemption limits.
  • Consider alternatives like PPF, SCSS, or ELSS for tax-efficient returns.

Key Takeaways

- FD interest is fully taxable as per your slab rate.
- TDS applies if interest exceeds the threshold.
- Submit Form 15G/15H to avoid unnecessary TDS if your income is below the exemption limit.
- Tax-Saving FDs give Section 80C deduction, but interest remains taxable.

Conclusion

Fixed Deposits are safe and reliable, but investors must account for tax liability on the interest earned. By planning wisely and using tax provisions effectively, you can maximize post-tax returns from your FDs.
